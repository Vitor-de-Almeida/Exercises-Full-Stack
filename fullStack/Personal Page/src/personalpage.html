Since I was young, I've always been fascinated by games. At 17, I started the Industrial Mechanical Engineering course at UTFPR, where I had my first contact with programming through the C++ language. It was during this period that I realized how much software development could unite logical reasoning, creativity, and purpose. Soon I discovered my true passion: web development. Learning HTML, CSS, and JavaScript was a watershed moment — each line of code represented a new way to create, innovate, and solve problems in a practical and visually impactful way.

Currently, I work at the Technology Directorate of the Military Police of the State of Paraná, acting as a software project manager. This role allows me to combine my institutional experience with technical learning, contributing to the digital modernization of the corporation. My main goal, however, is not just to advance in positions, but to evolve in knowledge and experience, continuously improving my skills and expanding my understanding of the technological universe. Today, more than an interest, technology represents for me a new vocation — proof that it's never too late to start over and turn a passion into a profession.

I am currently specializing in full-stack development, focusing on modern web technologies and best practices. This unique environment has taught me the importance of writing clean, maintainable code and implementing security-first approaches in software development.
